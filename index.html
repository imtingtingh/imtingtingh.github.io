About me

Fun

Jane's Walk is an global event that celebrates the life and ideas of urban activist Jane Jacobs. It involves free, citizen-led walking tours in cities around the world, where people explore their neighborhoods, and engage in discussions about local history, architecture, urban design, and community issues. Since 2019, the "一览众山小" (Yi Lan Zhong Shan Xiao) volunteer team has been organizing Jane's Walk events in China. With great success, we have orchestrated 20+ Jane's Walk in 9 cities, including Shanghai, Beijing, Shenzhen, and Guangzhou. These events cover a wide range of themes, such as 15-minute neighborhoods, walkable streets, urban renewal, biodiversity, inclusive communities, and waterfront spaces. "Yi Lan Zhong Shan Xiao" is a non-profit volunteer organization committed to advancing sustainable development goals in China. With a notable presence in China's sustainable urbanism and transportation media landscape, we exert significant influence in promoting sustainable practices and shaping the discourse surrounding urban sustainability and transportation.

As a volunteer with the organization, I co-organized a Jane's Walk in Shenzhen alongside fellow volunteers in July 2022. The city walk focused on the theme of "Connecting Communities with Nature". To delve into the design and significance of community parks, we embarked on immersive street walks, conducted insightful observations, and engaged in interviews with local residents. In a collaborative workshop, we collectively crafted a green map with a park-centric focus. Additionally, we were honored to have a distinguished landscape designer join us, sharing insights and facilitating a vibrant exchange of ideas. The event had over 30+ participants and garnered attention from local news media.
